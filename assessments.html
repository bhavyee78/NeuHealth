Reassessment at Transitions

Protecting Your Support as You Move Forward

Moving to a new school or college is a big step. Recent changes in government policy mean that many children with an EHCP may now need a reassessment when they change schools. We believe you shouldn't pay full price for an update.

⚠️ Don't Wait Until February!

By the time the Council sends the final EHCP plan in February, it is often too late to start a new assessment. Most families start their transition update with us in the Autumn Term (September–December) to stay safe.

Important EHCP Deadlines — 2026

📅 February 15th, 2026

For children moving from: Nursery to Primary, Infant to Junior (Year 3), Primary to Secondary (Year 7)

📅 March 31st, 2026

For young people moving from Secondary to Post-16 (College or Sixth Form)

If the Council misses these deadlines, they are breaking the law. A fresh assessment gives you the evidence you need to challenge them.

When do you need a Reassessment?

Reassessments (sometimes called “Phase Transfer Reviews”) are usually needed when moving:

  • → From Primary to Secondary School (Year 6)
  • → From Secondary to Post-16 College or Sixth Form
  • → When a school or Local Authority requests updated evidence to keep an EHCP active

EHCP & Education Support

The EHCP (Education, Health and Care Plan) process is often long and complex. We provide expert guidance on every document you will face.

Help with the initial request for an Education, Health and Care assessment from your Local Authority. We guide you through the evidence required and what to include to maximise your chances of acceptance.
Reviewing the legal wording of a proposed EHCP to ensure it meets your child's needs. A weak plan leads to weak provision — we make sure every section is legally enforceable.
Guidance on how to update your child's plan as they grow and their needs change. Annual reviews are your opportunity to strengthen the EHCP — we make sure you use it well.
Support if you disagree with a decision made by the Local Authority, including help with Tribal Appeal Forms and professional assistance with the paperwork needed for a legal appeal at SEND Tribunal.
Helping you understand the legal rules for naming a specific school in the EHCP, including how to appeal if the Local Authority refuses your preferred school.
Support with the primary document used to apply for Exam Access Arrangements (such as extra time, a scribe or a reader) for students with learning difficulties or disabilities. We also provide Reasonable Adjustments Templates.

Protecting Their Future: Trust Documents

For children with complex needs, planning for the future is a priority. We help you create a “safety net” for when you are no longer around.

Special Needs Trusts

Legal setups that hold money for your child without affecting their right to government benefits. Essential planning for long-term financial security.

Letters of Wishes

A guide for future carers on exactly how you want your child to be looked after — covering routines, preferences, medical needs and much more.

Wills & Inheritance

Ensuring your estate is handled in a way that protects a vulnerable child — avoiding unintended loss of benefits.

Power of Attorney & Deputyship

Guidance on who makes decisions when a child becomes an adult — including Court of Protection Deputyship and Lasting Power of Attorney.
