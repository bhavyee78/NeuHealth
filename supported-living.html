Our Mission

The 360-Degree Shield

We don't just “help out” — we teach the skills needed for a lifetime of independence. True independence is built on a foundation of safety, skills and justice. By combining high-level life coaching with expert legal advocacy, we ensure no young person is left behind and no parent is left to struggle alone.

How We Protect & Empower

1

The Road to Independence

We provide expert mentoring to help young adults gain independence, master life skills and financial literacy. From finding a first job to staying healthy and confident, we build the “daily habits” for a successful life.

2

Home & Family Help

We take the pressure off the household. Whether it is cleaning, meal prep, or taking children for nature walks to give parents a break, we ensure the home is a calm and happy place to live.

3

Legal Advocacy

With our unique legal background, we ensure the system treats you fairly. We provide free consultations, fight for the funding you deserve, and challenge the Council when they say “no.”

4

The “Invest to Save” Promise

Our weekly package is designed to save money in the long run by preventing expensive hospital stays and care home placements. Investing in your future today is the smartest move to make.

Our Core Pillars

Everything You Need in One Place

We can help you unlock “Specialist” funding pots. We aren't just a support service — we are a long-term investment.

Life Skills & Independence

Cooking, cleaning, travel and everything in between. We build the practical foundations for independent living.

  • Life Skills & Home Prep — cooking healthy meals, managing a budget, keeping a home clean and safe
  • Job & Education Mentoring — CV writing, interview practice, finding college courses and volunteer roles
  • Travel & Community Training — confidence to use buses and trains, finding local clubs and gyms
  • Practical Admin Support — reading mail, filling out forms, managing important appointments

Financial Literacy Programme

Debt help, budgeting and bills — designed specifically for neurodivergent minds. No jargon, just clear steps.

  • Week 1: The Safety Net — stopping new debt, finding where your money goes
  • Week 2: Debt Control — simple ways to pay off what you owe
  • Week 3: Money Systems — “autopilot” systems so you don't forget bills
  • Week 4: Earning More — side hustles and jobs that fit your skills
  • Week 5: Wage Strategy — making your paycheck last all month
  • Week 6: Future Growth — keeping your money growing forever

Education & Employment

CVs, college and job coaching — helping you find the right path and take the first steps with confidence.

  • Finding the Right Course — helping you identify local colleges and training you'll love
  • CV Building — listing skills and what makes you a great worker
  • Application Support — sitting with you to fill out job or volunteer forms
  • Practice Interviews — acting as the employer so you can practice your answers
  • Travel to Work/College — practicing the route until you feel safe going alone
  • Starting Small — finding volunteering or work experience to try before committing

Emotional Wellbeing

Confidence, mental health and community — because feeling good on the inside is just as important as being safe on the outside.

  • Building Confidence — celebrating the small wins, like going to the shop alone
  • Anxiety Support — a calm space to talk when the world feels too loud
  • Healthy Habits — how sleep, good food and fresh air make your brain feel happier
  • Friendships & Loneliness — finding local groups where you can meet like-minded people
  • Regular Mentoring — check-in chats to solve problems before they get too big

Care Leavers (16+)

  • Transition planning with your Local Authority Personal Adviser
  • Help using your “Setting Up Home” grant
  • Advocacy to ensure you receive all housing & financial support you're legally owed

Recovery Support

  • A stable, alcohol-and-drug-free space to stay on track
  • Replacing old habits with gym sessions, cooking or hobbies
  • Help getting to doctor or recovery group appointments on time

Young Parents

  • Parenting skills coaching for babies and toddlers
  • Baby-proofing and creating a warm, clean home
  • Finding childcare so you can return to college or work
